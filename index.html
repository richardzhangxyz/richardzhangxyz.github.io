About

Personal Projects

At UChicago, I conducted research at the intersection of human perception and machine learning with Alexander Todorov and Stefan Uddenberg at the Chicago Booth Perception and Judgement Lab.

We are currently publishing a paper that is being considered for submission to Psychological Science titled "Deep Models of Cars".
My research at the lab was rewarded a Honors Senior Thesis in Cognitive Science and I presented my findings at the 2024 UChicago Undergraduate Research Symposium. My poster was presented as part of the "Inquiries in Computer and Data Science Section": DEEP LEARNING MODELS OF OBJECT IMPRESSIONS: AUTOMOBILES AS A CASE STUDY.

I also worked as a Data Science Researcher at Fermilab on a Deep Learning Project to detect neutrino events.
The title of the experiment was: Real-time Tagging and Triggering of Neutrino Particles with Deep Learning Convolutional Neural Networks for Liquid Argon Time Projection Chambers.
